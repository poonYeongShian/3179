Sustainable Transport: Carbon dioxide Emissions & EV Sales

This data was sourced from the website 'Our World in Data,' created by Hannah Ritchie and Max Roser, which tracks CO2 emissions. It calculates the individual contribution of each country's average citizen by dividing the country's total emissions by its population. This calculation provides us with the metric of CO2 emissions per capita.

The above visualization demonstrates the disparities in per capita emissions worldwide. In this dataset of global Carbon dioxide emissions per capita, Qatar tops the list with 35.59 tons of CO2 emissions per capita, while the Democratic Republic of Congo has the lowest at 0.0272 tons, revealing significant global disparities.

Studies indicate that electric vehicles (EVs) generally contribute to reduced levels of greenhouse gas emissions (GHGs) compared to the average new gasoline-powered car. This effect becomes even more pronounced as electricity generation increasingly relies on renewable sources such as wind and solar power.

From 2015 to 2021, China has consistently held the title of having the highest electric vehicle sales.

Widespread charging infrastructure encourages more people to switch to EVs, thus reducing overall carbon emissions from the transportation sector.

From this point, it's evident that China continues to maintain the world's highest quantity of electric vehicle fast and slow charging stations.
